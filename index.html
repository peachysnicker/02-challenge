Minimal Lighting in Bedroom

As obvious as it may seem to want darkness in the bedroom to have a proper night of sleep, that is not always the case. Consider the bright street lamps outside of your house that could be potientially disrupting your sleep. What do you think about when you think of a dog den? Yes! Darkeness! Take some time to check your bedroom to see if there are any possibly light disruptors that could be potentially affecting your sleep.

Don't Eat too Much Food Before Bed

Just as your dog has a structured feeding schedule, so should you. It is best practice to limit late night snacks or even late dinners. Eating too close to when it is time to sleep can cause your body to work harder at digesting your food rather than sleeping. I love my late night treats but do yourself a favour and eat no closer than two hours before bedtime.

Read Before Bed

Put down the iphone! As much as we love to check our phone before sleep, possibly scrolling instagram or facebook, it is not a good habit! Instead of reading the web, pick up a book! Twenty minutes of reading can be all your body needs to settle down from the stressors of the day. You don't typically see dogs scrolling endlessly online instead of sleeping!

Why sleep like dogs?

You may be asking yourself why we should follow the sleeping habits of dogs? It can be any animal! We live busy lives and the number one priority we need to have is to get a proper nights rest.

Watch & Learn

Always observe those who are better than you at something to see what you can learn.

It's Free!

The best of all is that it costs nothing to observe a dog sleeping and learn from it! What are you waiting for? Start now at the dog nearest you!
